# Introduction for lecturers

Welcome to the official guide for using **FAILS** (Fancy Automated Internet Lecture System). FAILS is an open-source, browser-based platform designed for interactive and complex lectures in academic settings.

## 💡 What is FAILS?
FAILS is optimized for teaching methods requiring real-time calculation presentation and dynamic content delivery, making it ideal for subjects such as natural sciences (especially theoretical physics and mathematics).

### Key Features at a Glance
*   **Interactive Tools:** Built-in support for polls, live chat, and annotations.
*   **Multi-Display Support:** Supports output across multiple projectors or smartboards.
*   **LMS Integration:** Seamlessly integrated with Learning Management Systems (LMSs), including Moodle, via the LTI interface.
*   **Content Versatility:** Handles images, PDF backgrounds.
*   **Media Streaming:** Enables live audio and video transmission to remote participants.
*   **Interactive visualization:** Supports Jupyter lite based python apps, for interactive plots or datascience visualizations.

## 🚀 Getting Started: The FAILS app
The first point of interaction is the **FAILS app**, located within your LMS course activity. This dashboard serves as the central hub for planning, organizing, and configuring your lecture material and starting your lecture.

### Accessing the FAILS app as activity in your LMS
1.  **Login:** Log into your school or university's LMS (e.g. Moodle).
2.  **Navigation:** Navigate to the desired course section.
3.  **Activity Selection:** Add a new activity and select **FAILS**.
4.  **Launch Prep:** Select `save and display` to open the **FAILS app** for preparation or starting your lecture.

### FAILS app (Preparation Tasks)
The dashboard is organized into several modular panels:

*   **Start/Join Lecture:** The primary module for beginning the lecture.
*   **PDF Script & Backgrounds:** Upload images (`Upload picture` button) or PDF files to serve as backgrounds, allowing you to annotate pre-prepared material during the live lecture.
*   **Notebook Management:** Lecturers can upload and edit **Jupyter Lite Notebooks**. Selected individual cells within these notebooks can be utilized directly as interactive apps during the lecture. The notebook can also be shown and run during the lecture,
*   **Other Lectures Panel:** Allows importing materials, polls, and pictures from previous classes or years of lectures using a designated `Get...` button (e.g., `Get Polls`, `Get Pictures`).
