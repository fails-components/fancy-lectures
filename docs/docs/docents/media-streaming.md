# Multimedia Streaming: Audio, Video, and External Content
FAILS is capable of transmitting multimedia content, enabling lecture participation  even when the lecturer or students are remote.

## 🔴 Live Audio/Video Transmission (Remote Presence)
This feature ensures students can participate in a lecture regardless of their physical location.

### Enabling Streaming
1.  **Initiation:** On the lecture welcome pop-up, activate the audio/video functionality via its dedicated button (or through the Options Menu on the moving toolbox).
2.  **A/V Routers:** The system connects to institutional A/V routers globally. Transmission is transport and fully end-to-end encrypted. Routing uses pseudonymized information, ensuring student privacy even if staudents or global routers are in hostile environments.

### Controlling Media Streams
| Stream | Control Button | Action & Best Practice |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Video broadcasting** | Camera button | Activates video transmission, the pop-up allows to select the video input device. Background removal is available, the background is replaced either by a single color or blurred. |
|  **Video reception** | Monitor botton |  Use the monitor button to view the transmitted video. |
| **Audio broadcasting** | Microphone Icon | Transmits your voice. Check the left-hand side of the monitor for a live audio level gauge. The pop-up allows to select the video input device. |
| **Speaker Output** | Speaker Icon  | Allows you to hear remote participants or students from other rooms/devices. The pop-up allows to select the audio output device.  |

## 🖥️ Screen Sharing External Sources
For lectures that rely on traditional presentation software (e.g., PowerPoint, Keynote), FAILS has integrated screensharing capabilities:

1.  **Initiation:** Click the **screensharing button**. Select whether to share (a portion of) your main screen or an external video input device.
2.  **Combined Viewing:** The system is designed to automatically switch focus (via motion detection) between the shared presentation and FAILS' native handwriting, giving students automatically the correct feed. The students can also switch manually between screen sharing and FAILS' main blackboard.

## 📡 Summary of Student Experience
When A/V is enabled:
*   **Student Access:** Students can press a dedicated "Join AV" button on their devices. They have control over whether they join with audio and video or with audio-only or video-only to manage bandwidth.
*   **Video Questions:** Students can send direct questions which appear as visible message boxes on the lecturer's screen. The lecture can then grant permission for direct audio and/or video participation of the asking student.
